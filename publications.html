SPIE PAPER NO. 12678-43 // 2025
Space-qualification of a commercial camera package with a Sony IMX455 CMOS detector
UV/Optical/IR Space Telescopes XII
Abstract. This work evaluates the flight readiness of a commercial Sony IMX455 detector for the CuRIOS-ED mission. We present the structural design of kinematic optical mounts and results of thermal-vacuum (TVAC) cycling from −40°C to +80°C. The study confirms that COTS hardware, integrated with custom shock-mitigation interfaces, can achieve space-grade performance — reducing mission detector costs by roughly 100× versus bespoke alternatives.
SPIE PAPER NO. 12180-153 // 2024
CuRIOS-ED: the technology demonstrator for the CubeSats for Rapid Infrared and Optical Surveys
Space Telescopes and Instrumentation
Abstract. CuRIOS-ED is the technology demonstrator for the CubeSats for Rapid Infrared and Optical Surveys mission. This paper details the mechanical design of the telescope payload — specifically the deployable baffle mechanism for 16U stowage — validated through nonlinear FEA and 12G vibration profiles, demonstrating effective stray-light rejection for time-domain astrophysics.
AAS MEETING ABSTRACTS // 2025
Advantages of a Space-Based, All-Sky Survey for Time-Domain Astrophysics
American Astronomical Society
Abstract. We present the scientific case for space-based infrared monitoring to overcome ground-based limits such as water-vapor absorption and the diurnal cycle, arguing for a distributed constellation of low-cost CubeSats enabling continuous observation of transient events — including gravitational-wave counterparts — in the infrared.
